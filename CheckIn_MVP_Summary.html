CheckIn — Personal Check-in App

A notification-driven, offline-first Android app for capturing your body state and emotion in seconds, with one-tap CSV export. Fully local — no accounts, no cloud, no sync.

MVP complete · all 11 build steps done · verified end-to-end on device

What this means for the user

🔔

Reminders that actually arrive

Set one or more daily times. The app nudges you with a notification — and keeps working reliably even after a phone restart or a time-zone change.

👆

Tap the notification, you're already checking in

No menus, no home screen to wade through. Tapping the reminder drops you straight onto the first question.

⚡

A whole check-in in seconds — no typing

Four quick body sliders (energy, tiredness, hunger, tension), then a colorful emotion wheel to pick exactly how you feel. Save. Done.

🎡

An emotion wheel that goes deep

Six core feelings open into finer and finer shades, so you can name the precise emotion instead of settling for "good" or "bad".

📒

Your history, neatly grouped

Every check-in is listed by day (Today / Yesterday / …). Tap one to correct it, or swipe to delete — with a gentle "undo" if it was a mistake.

📤

Export everything as a spreadsheet

One tap writes all your check-ins to a CSV file in your Downloads folder — yours to open in Excel, share, or archive.

🔒

Private by design — and safe across phones

Nothing ever leaves the device. When you switch phones, Android's backup carries your check-ins and reminder settings over for you.

data/alarm/AlarmScheduler.kt · AlarmTime.kt added

Inexact, Doze-proof alarms — no exact-alarm permission. Reliable delivery on aggressive OEMs comes from setAndAllowWhileIdle plus a battery-optimization exemption prompt, not from exact alarms (a deliberate product decision: ~15 min drift is fine). Each alarm is one-shot and re-armed after it fires. The next-occurrence math is extracted into the pure, unit-tested AlarmTime.

alarmManager.setAndAllowWhileIdle(
    AlarmManager.RTC_WAKEUP,
    AlarmTime.nextOccurrenceMillis(reminder.hour, reminder.minute),
    alarmPendingIntent(reminder),       // stable requestCode = id.hashCode()
)
notification/BootReceiver.kt · AlarmReceiver.kt added

Survives reboots, updates and clock changes. Alarms do not persist across a restart, so BootReceiver listens for BOOT_COMPLETED, MY_PACKAGE_REPLACED, TIME_SET and TIMEZONE_CHANGED, then re-arms every reminder (RescheduleRemindersUseCase via goAsync + coroutine). AlarmReceiver posts the notification and immediately schedules tomorrow's. Verified with a real device reboot — the alarm came back, exactly once.

presentation/checkin/ (CheckinViewModel, EmotionWheel.kt) added

MVI flow + a hand-drawn wheel. A single ViewModel drives both steps, mirroring in-progress fields to SavedStateHandle so a check-in survives process death. The wheel is a custom Canvas (annular-sector paths, two-stage bloom animated below the recomposition layer, rotated labels via TextMeasurer) and honours the system "reduce motion" setting. It is the single vivid element in an otherwise calm Material 3 palette.

MainActivity.kt · presentation/navigation/Routes.kt added

Type-safe Navigation with notification-aware routing. On launch the start destination is chosen from onboarding state and the launch intent; a notification tap deep-links straight into the check-in and, after save, finishes the activity to return the user where they were.
